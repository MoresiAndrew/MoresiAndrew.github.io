Computer Science Algorithmic Design and Programming I This course provides experience in developing algorithms, designing, implementing programs. Topics include syntax/semantics, flow control, loops, recursion, I/O, arrays, strings and pointers. 2017 Spring A+ 4 Introductory programming projects
Algorithmic Design and Programming II A study of fundamental techniques and algorithms for representing and manipulating data structures. Topics include data abstraction, recursion, stacks, queues, linked lists, trees, efficient methods of sorting and searching, and Big-O analysis. 2018 Fall C- 4 Programs involving pointers, recursion, stacks, etc.
Intro to Logic Systems Basic tools, methods and procedures to design combinational and sequential digital circuits and systems, including number systems, boolean algebra, logic minimization, circuit design, memory elements, and finite state machine design. 2018 Fall C 3 Understanding of digital circuits, logic systems, cirguit design, etc.
Math Calculus III Vectors, solid analytic geometry, calculus of several variables. Math Reasoning Proficiency course. 2019 Spring C+ 3 Developed understanding of Calculus
Interest Theory This course covers the concepts underlying the theory of interest and their applications to valuation of various cash flows, annuities certain, bonds, and loan repayment. This course is designed to help students prepare for Society of Actuaries exam FM (Financial Mathematics). It is oriented towards problem solving techniques applied to real-life situations and illustrated with previous exam problems. 2019 Fall C+ 3 Developed an understanding of the fundementals of interest on bonds, annuities, cash flows, loan repayment, etc.
History of Mathematics This is a history course with mathematics as its subject. Includes topics in the history of mathematics from early civilizations onwards. The growth of mathematics, both as an abstract discipline and as a subject which interacts with others and with practical concerns, is explored. Prerequisites or Cirequisite: MATH 2300 or MATH 2340. 2020 Spring B 3 Writing assignments aimed at figuring the thought process of ancient Mathematicians
History The Ancient Greek World Political and social institutions, intellectual life of Greek city-states to time of Alexander. 2019 Fall A- 3 Various writing assignments about the Ancient Greek world
